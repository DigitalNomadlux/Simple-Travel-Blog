Traveling with Your K-9 Companion

"Man’s Best Friend”
Who wouldn’t love to travel with their dog? It only seems natural that we would want our four-legged companions to travel alongside with us in our adventures and trips. Although, traveling with a K-9 can really be a "ultimate test" of their obedience and skills and can also come with quite some challenges. So, what's the best way you can make sure your trips are smooth and enjoyable for both parties? Well here are a few key things to keep in mind while getting started with your preparations.





Training Essentials

Traveling with your K-9 companion requires proper training and preparation in order for it to be a successful trip. Condition your dog for handling new and exciting environments. This ensures your dog’s safety along the way to your destination. Implementing positive reinforcement methods and a wide array of training is required in order to prepare your dog for travel. A dog who is well-socialized and well-mannered tends to travel the best. Hopefully some of these tips will help you travel better with your furry K-9 friend!

Airline Travel

First an most important, do not forget your pets passport! It is just as important as your own passport and will be required when flying to other countries. A pet passport is the collection of documents that you will need to import your pet to any foreign country. You can find airline pet policies commercial airlines that will fly your pet worldwide. Before making your reservation, be sure and know your airline's policies for flying pets so there are no surprises at check in. Being prepared is key to traveling with your pet. Give yourself plenty of time to get to your gate. Keep your accessories to a minimum to ensure you have free hands to handle your pet at security checkpoints.

Travel Preparation Tips

Make sure you pet is properly vaccinated. It does not matter how you are traveling or where you are staying; vaccinations are very important and help to keep your pet safe. Also, consider Microchipping your pet as this can be very helpful. Every pet owners worst nightmare is losing their pet let alone losing it in another country or an unfamiliar place. So for your own peace of mind, and to meet certain requirements for some countries and states, Microchipping is something worth asking your vet about. Otherwise, travel with a plan and most importantly.. have fun! Go see the world and experience it with you K-9 best friend!
